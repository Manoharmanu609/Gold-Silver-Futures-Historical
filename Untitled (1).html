import pandas as pd
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Load datasets
gold = pd.read_csv("Gold Futures Historical Data.csv")
silver = pd.read_csv("Silver Futures Historical Data.csv")

# Convert Date column
gold['Date'] = pd.to_datetime(gold['Date'])
silver['Date'] = pd.to_datetime(silver['Date'])

# Clean Price column (remove commas)
gold['Price'] = gold['Price'].astype(str).str.replace(',', '').astype(float)
silver['Price'] = silver['Price'].astype(str).str.replace(',', '').astype(float)

# Sort by Date
gold = gold.sort_values('Date')
silver = silver.sort_values('Date')

# Merge datasets on Date
merged = pd.merge(gold[['Date', 'Price']], 
                  silver[['Date', 'Price']], 
                  on='Date', 
                  suffixes=('_Gold', '_Silver'))

# Plot comparison
plt.figure()

plt.plot(merged['Date'], merged['Price_Gold'])
plt.plot(merged['Date'], merged['Price_Silver'])

plt.title("Gold vs Silver Price Comparison")
plt.xlabel("Date")
plt.ylabel("Price")
plt.legend(["Gold", "Silver"])

plt.xticks(rotation=45)
plt.grid(True)

plt.show()

Results of Gold and Silver Comparison

⃣ Trend Analysis

Both Gold and Silver prices show an overall upward trend over the long term. However, Gold shows more stable growth compared to Silver.

⃣ Volatility Comparison

Silver prices fluctuate more frequently and sharply.

Gold is comparatively more stable and less volatile.

Silver = High Risk, High FluctuationGold = Stable Investment

Conclusion

Gold is safer and more stable for long-term investment.

Silver offers higher short-term opportunities but with higher risk.
